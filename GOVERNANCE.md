# Governance

_GRU953 Markdown_

GRU953 Markdown uses lightweight, open governance. The aim is to keep decisions
transparent and low-friction while protecting the quality and direction of the
project.

## Roles

- **Contributor** — Anyone who opens an issue, proposes a change, or submits a
  pull request. No prior commitment is required.
- **Committer** — A trusted contributor with a sustained track record who may
  review and merge changes within an agreed area.
- **Maintainer** — Responsible for the overall health of the project: releases,
  roadmap, and final review of significant changes.
- **Steering** — Sets long-term direction and resolves disputes that cannot be
  settled at the maintainer level.

## Decision-making: lazy consensus

Most decisions are made by **lazy consensus**. A proposal is considered
accepted if no reasoned objection is raised within **7 days** of being posted.
Anyone may register support explicitly to speed this up. A reasoned objection
moves the proposal to active discussion until it is resolved.

## RFC lifecycle

Substantial changes — to the conversion pipeline, the application architecture,
or governance itself — follow a Request for Comments (RFC) process:

1. **Draft** — The author writes up the proposal and opens it for review.
2. **Discussion** — Open for community comment for a minimum of **14 days**.
3. **Final comment period** — A **7-day** window to raise any last concerns
   before a decision.
4. **Accepted / Rejected** — The maintainer records the outcome. Accepted RFCs
   move to implementation; rejected RFCs are archived with the reasoning noted.

## Resolving ties

Where consensus cannot be reached, the matter is decided by a **majority vote
of Steering**. The decision and its rationale are recorded publicly.
